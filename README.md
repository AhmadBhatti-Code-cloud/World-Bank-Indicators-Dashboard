# World-Bank-Indicators-Dashboard
This repository contains a Power BI dashboard built on World Bank data, providing insights into economic and demographic indicators across countries and regions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Key Visuals :


- GDP (USD)

- Population density (people per sq. km)

- Unemployment rate

- Birth rate (per 1000 people)

- Electric power consumption (kWh over time)

- Average life expectancy by region


------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Questions :

Q1 : What is the average GDP across all countries, and how does it relate to population density?

Q2 : How does the birth rate vary across income groups?

Q3 : What is the trend in electric power consumption over time, and what might this suggest about development?

Q4 : How does life expectancy differ by region, and which region has seen the highest improvement?

Q5 : What is the unemployment rate, and how does it vary across regions or income groups?


------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Solutions :

Answer1 : We analyzed GDP by comparing it to population density and income groups.

Answer2 : Birth rates were broken down by income group, showing a trend: lower income countries have higher birth rates.

Answer3 : Electric power consumption over time suggests steady growth in energy use.

Answer4 : Life expectancy differed by Region, with North America and Europe seeing the highest values.

Answer5 : The unemployment rate was higher in some regions such as South Asia compared to others.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
    
Recommendations

- Focus on energy efficiency improvements, especially in high growth economies.

- Invest in healthcare in low-income countries to improve life expectancy.

- Examine birth rates in relation to economic growth to understand demographic shifts.
